Airhead
A low cost, self powered air quality sensor for the future.

Our Philosphy

We believe that an important part of solving this problem is to have a cheap method of obtaining accurate and real time data on the extent of the crisis. Policy makers can devise and legislate new regulations more effectively if they have hard evidence for on the country-wide trends in air pollution. Law enforcement officials can then test the efficacy of these solutions by using a data driven approach to curbing emissions.

More than 80% of people living in urban areas that monitor air pollution are exposed to air quality levels that exceed the World Health Organisation limits. Populations in low-income cities are the most impacted; 98% of cities in low- and middle income countries with more than 100,000 inhabitants do not meet World Health Organisation air quality guidelines. However, in high income countries, that percentage decreases to 56%.

For example, NO2 pollution, which is produced largely by diesel vehicles, causes 5,900 early deaths every year in London. Most air quality zones across the UK break legal limits and the crisis was called a “public health emergency” by MPs in April. Scientists said that one in 10 cases of Alzheimer’s in people living near busy roads could be linked to air pollution.

What makes it tick?

How does the worlds more scalable and affordable air quality device actually work?

Setup

Setup is painless. The Airhead powers itself using Solar and batteries. Automatically connects for transmission and starts sensing.

Gather Data

Once the Airhead is setup, it will begin reading data at set intervals from the environment.

Transmit

The data can be transmitted over a local network, or long distances using a low power radio network.

Analyse

View realtime readings from all your sensors as well as their histories. Analyse differents between sensors depending on location.

What does it do?

There air is full of a lot of stuff! Airhead detects the 'stuff' most important to you. It is packed full of sensors to calculate the quality of the air, and send the results to the cloud.

The core of our product is the MiCS-4514 module, which measures the concentration of Nitrogen Dioxide (NO2) and Carbon Monoxide (CO). The module has 2 sensor chips with independent heating and sensitive layers. Once chip measures oxidising gases (OX) while the other measures reducing gases (RED). The heat produced by the heating element causes the surrounding gas to react with the sensitive layer, and the change in the layer’s electrical conductivity tells us the identity of the gas and its concentration in ppm.
